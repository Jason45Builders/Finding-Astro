Before anything else

Get set up in three steps

This is the only part that's a strict sequence — everything after this you can explore in any order.

1

Create your account

Sign up with any email — no invitation or approval needed to start as a citizen volunteer.

2

Turn on availability

Go to Profile → Responder Settings. Flip on Available for Rescue, set a response radius and your transport type. This is what puts you in the pool for nearby cases — leave it off and you won't be matched to anything.

Open Respond SOS

This is your main working screen from now on — a live list and map of open cases near you, sorted by priority.

The core loop

Responding to a case

Every case you claim moves through the same six-stage pipeline. Update your status as you go — it's what lets everyone else, including whoever reported it, see the case is actually moving.

Claimed You tap Claim
En Route Heading there
On Scene Arrived
Picked Up In transport
At Hospital Handed off
Completed Case closed
Can't finish? Abandon the case with a short reason instead of leaving it stuck — it goes back into the open pool so someone else can pick it up. No penalty for being honest about it.

You can add notes at any stage, and log photos as proof of pickup and treatment. Case timelines are visible to whoever reported it, so they can see it's being handled without needing to call anyone.

You're not only a responder

Reporting a case yourself

If you spot an animal in distress while you're out, you file it the same way any citizen would — from the Emergency page, no login required for the report itself.

Report an Animal in Distress

Pin the location (GPS-detected), attach a photo, describe the condition, and pick a severity. That's it — it broadcasts to nearby available responders immediately.

Critical Serious Stable

Not an emergency? Use the Regular Report tab instead for lost pets, ABC/sterilization requests, or ongoing conflicts — same form, no severity picker.

Institutional integrity

Where the money goes

No individual ever collects money for a rescue. Every payout is released directly to the treating clinic, and only after three things are true.

01

Proof submitted

Bills and treatment records are on file for the case.

02

Verified

Confirmed by the treating clinic or hospital directly.

03

Funds released

Paid to the clinic's account — never to a volunteer or reporter.

You can see this in practice on the Funding page — every treatment case shows what's been raised against what it costs, before and after your involvement.

Staying available (or not)

Three settings control whether — and how — you show up in the matching pool.

Available for Rescue

The master switch. Off means you get no case notifications at all — toggle it whenever your day changes.

Response Radius

How far you're notified about — from 1 to 50 km. Smaller means fewer, more relevant alerts.

Transport Type

On foot, bike, car, or van — this helps dispatch match you to cases you can actually carry out.

Notifications

The bell icon in the header collects everything — new alerts, status updates on cases you're following.

Quick answers

Before you ask

Do I need to be available all the time?

No. Toggle Available for Rescue off in Profile any time — you'll simply stop receiving case notifications until you turn it back on.

What if I claim a case and can't finish it?

Abandon it with a short reason from the case screen. It returns to the open pool immediately so someone else can claim it — there's no penalty for reporting honestly.

The animal is wildlife, not a stray — same process?

No — use Wildlife Rescue instead. It walks you through species-specific do/don't guidance before you do anything, since handling wildlife incorrectly can be dangerous for both of you.

Where does donated funding actually go?

Directly to the verified treating clinic, and only after proof of treatment is confirmed. No volunteer or reporter ever handles the money.

Can I report something without creating an account?

Yes, for emergencies — the Emergency report form doesn't require login. You'll want an account to claim, respond to, or track any case afterward though.
